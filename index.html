About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Scientific Computing, Modeling & Simulation at Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU), India, specializing in molecular-scale investigations of biological systems with a focus on protein-mediated cell signaling, drug delivery and rational drug design. My research integrates first-principles calculations, molecular dynamics simulations and advanced data processing and analysis to unravel complex biomolecular mechanisms. I have worked on theoretical and simulation-based studies including interactions of organometallic complexes with biomolecules, Ru-DNA systems, collagen–integrin (α-2A domain) interactions and the anti-inflammatory action mechanism of curcumin. I am familiar with a range of machine learning techniques, including robust data preprocessing, regression modeling and feature selection methods to iteratively improve predictive performance. My academic experience includes visiting PhD appointments at International Institute of Information Technology(IIIT) Hyderabad and University of Mumbai, as well as an academic visit to Uppsala University funded by the Swedish Research Council. I am keen to extend my PhD work through interdisciplinary collaborations and interactions, with long-term interests in pharmaceutical R&D, data science and materials science research environments.

Research Experience

  • Academic Visit – Materials Theory
    Visiting researcher at Uppsala University , Sweden, funded by the Swedish Research Council . The visit focused on extending Ph.D. research through theoretical and computational approaches relevant to molecular interactions and materials modeling.
  • Ph.D. Research – Computational Biophysics & Drug Design
    Theoretical investigation of organometallic complex–biomolecule interactions using molecular dynamics simulations to elucidate drug–target interaction mechanisms. Key studies include Ru-complex interaction with calf thymus DNA, collagen binding with the α-2A domain of integrin, and the molecular action mechanism of curcumin as an anti-inflammatory agent. Parts of this work were carried out at the International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad , and the University of Mumbai , involving extensive use of simulation and force-field development tools.
  • Project Assistant – Machine Learning for Materials Discovery
    Applied machine learning methodologies for predicting thermoelectric material properties, incorporating data preprocessing, regression models, and feature selection techniques to incrementally enhance predictive accuracy, with transferable relevance to data-driven drug discovery.
  • M.Phil. Dissertation – First-Principles Materials Modeling
    Conducted density functional theory–based computational studies on II–VI semiconducting materials for ferromagnetism using the VASP package, strengthening expertise in electronic structure calculations.
  • M.Sc. Project – Materials Synthesis
    Prepared nanocrystalline ZnS using a novel chemical bath deposition route, gaining foundational experience in materials synthesis and structure–property relationships.
